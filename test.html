Logarithms

In this section, $a$ and $b$ represent positive real numbers. $b$ is referred to as "the base" and we restrict $b\neq 1$.

Definition. Logarithims ($\log$) form a family of functions of various bases defined so that the following two forms are equivalent and convey the same information.

Exponential Form

$$a^b = c$$ $$\text{“$a$ to the $b$ equals $c$"}$$

Logarithmic Form

$$\log_b c = a$$ $$\text{“$\log$ base $b$ of $c$ equals $a$"}$$
$$\log_b xy = \log_b x + \log_b y$$ $$\text{ for $x,y>0$}$$
$$\log_b \left( \frac{x}{y} \right) = \log_b x - \log_b y$$ $$\text{ for $x,y>0$}$$

Domain and Range

$y=\log_b x$ has a domain of $(0,\infty)$ and range of $(-\infty, \infty)$. Of important note, $\log_b x$ is undefined for any value of $x$ such that $x \leq 0$.
$$\log_b 1 = 0$$
$$\log_b (x^y) = y \log_b x$$ $$\text{ for $x>0$}$$

Change of Base Rule

$$\log_{b_1} x = \frac{\log_{b_2} x }{\log_{b_2} b_1}$$ $$\text{for $x > 0$}$$

Logarithms as Inverses

$$ \cancel b^{\cancel{\log_b} x} = x \text{ for $x > 0$}$$ $$\bcancel \log_b {\bcancel b}^x = x \text{ for all $x$}$$ In other words, if $f(x) = b^x$, then the inverse function is $f^{-1}(x) = \log_b x$.

Special Notation

$$\log x \text{ means } \log_{10} x$$ $$\ln x \text{ means } \log_e x$$ $ e \approx 2.718281828$ (Euler's number)
